A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

He is Missile Man of India

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, in full Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, (born October 15, 1931, Rameswaram, India—died July 27, 2015, Shillong), Indian scientist and politician who played a leading role in the development of India’s missile and nuclear weapons programs.

Few lines about A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

  • A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, in full Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, Indian scientist and politician who played a leading role in the development of India’s missile and nuclear weapons programs.
  • He was born on October 15, 1931, Rameswaram, India, Tamil Nadu state.
  • His father’s name was Jainulabdeen Marakayar and his mother’s name was Ashiamma Jainulabdeen.
  • He was unmarried.He is missile man of India.
  • He served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007 and was widely regarded as the "People's President."
  • Kalam was also known as the "Missile Man of India" for his work on the development of ballistic missile technology.
  • He was a prolific author and wrote many books, including "Wings of Fire," an autobiography that detailed his life and career.
  • Kalam received numerous awards and honors throughout his life, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor, in 1997.
  • He was a strong advocate for education and spent much of his later years promoting the importance of science and technology education in India.
  • Awards and Honors:
    * Bharat Ratna: In 1997, Kalam received India's highest civilian honor, the Bharat Ratna, for his contributions to the field of science and technology.
    * Padma Vibhushan: In 1990, Kalam was awarded the Padma Vibhushan, the second-highest civilian award in India, for his work in defense research and development.
    * King Charles II Medal: In 2007, Kalam was awarded the King Charles II Medal by the Royal Society, UK, for his contributions to science and engineering.
  • Kalam was a strong supporter of India's nuclear program and played a key role in the country's 1998 nuclear tests.
  • Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong.
  • Summary:
    In 2021, the Indian government announced that Kalam's birthday, October 15, would be celebrated as "World Students' Day" in honor of his dedication to education.
